Everything You Need to Know About Web Accessibility

You know that internet is one of the most important tools in the world to keep oneself updated. It is the largest source of learning and communication. According to internet world stats in 2020 there are 4.83 billion users on the internet. This is about 62% of the world population. This means that every 3 out of 5 people are using Internet. Every company is having its website. People are getting jobs on the internet, students study from the resources on the internet, people send money online, the information and complaining system of most of the private companies and governments are online, most of the people buy things online. Millions of people have internet as the only source of earning. In this decade, we cannot even imagine life without internet.

Web Accessibility refers to the accessibility of a website to all people regardless of their any kind of impairment or disability. It is responsibility of the people who design and develop websites or online applications that their website facilitates all types of audience even if they are having any kind of disability. According to the Americans with Disabilities Act(ADA) the website should entertain the disabled people in the same way in which the people without disabilities are entertained to remove discrimination. The disabled audience include people who are completely blind, partially blind, color blind, physically restricted to move, deaf, people who hear louder, people who cannot speak or have difficulty to speak like stammering, etc.

WE can make web accessible to everyone by following Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) which includes:
• Making the website fully navigate able and access able by using the keyboard only.
• Using the colors that can be seen easily by color blind.
• For all non text content adding text alternatives do that deaf people can access that content.
• For all text content there also can be added audio alternative for blind people.
• Using headings in right way to improve readability and organize data properly.
• Even if the CSS does not load the logical flow of the website should be maintained using HTML.
• There are many website having forms for feedback these can also be made in a way so that   disable people can fill them without any problem.

If all the above things are added in a website then the website will be a lot more easy to be accessed by all kind of audience, and it will also increase the views of our site and provide a better user interface.
